      Let's have a look at describing gas-liquid mass transfer. The mathematical formulation of the volumetric (reactor based) mass transfer rate is given by:
      ​
      $$r_{O_2}^{mt}=k_La \cdot \left(C_{O_2}^{sat}-C_{O_2} \right)$$
      --------
      $$ \left[ \frac{mol\,O_2}{L\cdot day}  \right] = \left[ \frac{1}{day} \cdot \frac{mol\,O_2}{L}  \right]$$
      ​
      Note that $r_{O_2}^{mt}$ is a volume based rate. The rate is defined by a mass transfer coefficient ($k_La$) multiplied by a concentration difference between the saturated oxygen concentration ($C_{O_2}^{sat}$) and the oxygen concentration in the fermenter ($C_{O_2}$). The saturated oxygen concentration is dependent on the oxygen partial pressure and typically varies between $6-8 \frac{mg}{L}$ (1.9e-4 to 2.5e-4 $\frac{mol\,O_2}{L}$) at atmospheric air conditions The chemical engineering approach is to assume that all liquid on the gas-liquid interface (edge of bubbles) are saturated (at $C_{O_2}^{sat}$ ) while $C_{O_2}$ will always be less than $C_{O_2}^{sat}$. The mass transfer coefficient (note the units) is determined by the air flowrate, sparger and impeller characteristics but will be supplied in this course.
